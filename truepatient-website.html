Why CNNs for Cancer Detection?

Blood smear analysis requires identifying subtle morphological abnormalities in cell shape, nucleus size, and staining patterns. CNNs are uniquely suited for this — they learn hierarchical visual features directly from thousands of labeled examples.

Our models are trained on curated public medical datasets from Kaggle, covering Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia and multiple other blood cancer subtypes. We apply data augmentation, class balancing, and transfer learning from ImageNet-pretrained weights.

The VLM layer then takes these predictions and generates natural language explanations — a first in affordable diagnostic tooling for India.
